the year was 1995.

Heads turned as Gary Kremen stepped into his office, dressed in a rainbow tie-dyed tee. The founder of online dating site Match.com, still in its infancy at the time, had arrived for his first television interview, conducted while perched comfortably in a beanbag chair. It was during this infamous Q&A that the CEO would unwittingly predict the future. He looked into the camera and, making eye contact with the audience watching at home, uttered words more truth than he ever could have realized:

"Match.com will bring more love to the planet than anything since Jesus Christ."

Nearby, Match.com communications director was stunned; surely, she thought, this would be the moment to end the company for good.
But as time went on, she, along with the the rest of the world, came to realize...

Kremen was right.

Over the course of the next 20 years, what began as a last-resort for the lonely cat women of the 90s would transform into one of the most innovative, exciting, convenient, frustrating, reinvigorating, mind-boggling tools for 21st-century singles:
online dating.

...And that was only the beginning.

As online dating became destigmatized and the number of users grew, Match.com was joined by a variety of cohorts. One of the most famous, eHarmony, arrived in August 2000, founded by clinical psychologist Dr. Neil Clark Warren and his son-in law. Known for its lengthy application process, including a questionnaire that once included 450 questions, it became the first algorithm-based dating site. At its height, the service boasted 33 million users. In 2015, it was reported that 71% of women and 69% of men met their spouse within a year of creating an eHarmony account.

A look into the current state of the online dating scene.

who?

Despite a rise in the popularity of dating apps since Match.com's 1994, relatively few Americans log on to find love. A 2017 survey found that only 6% of Americans used dating apps. Meanwhile, 45.2% of Americans aged 18 years or older reported being currently single. Interesting.

According to a 2017 report, most online dating service users are usually on-the-go. The survey found that 76% of users used a smartphone to access their favorite online dating websites and apps. Convenience, as always, is key.

why?

People using online dating services tend to log on for a variety of reasons, and sometimes more than one reason at once. According to data from April 2017, the majority of surveyed users reported using online dating for three reasons: to meet people who shared their interests, to meet people who shared their values or to find someone for a long-term relationship or marriage. Honorable mention: those searching for "fun" came in at 26%.
